Polio Surveillance (AFP)

NoteAFP Surveillance Context

Acute flaccid paralysis (AFP) surveillance is the global standard for detecting poliovirus circulation. South Africa maintains high AFP surveillance sensitivity as part of polio eradication efforts. The target is ≥2 AFP cases per 100,000 children under 15 years.

NoteInterpretation Notes

EPI-targeted VPDs:

  • Measles/Rubella: Core EPI vaccines; rising cases may indicate immunisation gaps
  • DTP diseases (Diphtheria, Pertussis, Tetanus): Rare in well-immunised populations
  • Polio surveillance: AFP cases should be investigated for poliovirus; target ≥2/100k <15 yrs
  • Hib: Haemophilus influenzae type B; vaccine introduced in 1999

Surveillance implications:

  • Clusters of measles cases warrant immunisation response
  • Any diphtheria or poliomyelitis case is a public health emergency
  • High CFR in certain VPDs may reflect late presentation or case ascertainment bias
