Opportunities for Linkage

Completeness of patient identifiers and SA ID number validity across NMC notifications

NoteAbout this Page

This page reports on the quality of patient identifier fields that enable record linkage across health information systems.

Identifier Column Purpose
SA ID Number patient_id_no Links to Home Affairs, NHLS, vital registration
Hospital Folder No. folder_no Links to facility-level clinical records
MRN mrn (if present) Medical Record Number from facility HIS

Completeness classification:

  • Present — a non-empty value was recorded (excluding “Unknown”)
  • Missing — field is NA, empty string, whitespace-only, or recorded as “Unknown”

SA ID validation applies two sequential checks to IDs classified as “present”:

  1. Correct length — a valid SA ID is exactly 13 numeric digits. IDs with letters, spaces, or wrong length fail this check.
  2. Luhn checksum — the last digit is a checksum computed from the first 12; IDs that fail this are likely transcription errors (digit swaps, typos).

NoteWhat counts as “Missing”?

A SA ID is classified as missing if the patient_id_no field is:

  • NA (null / not recorded)
  • Empty string ("")
  • Contains only whitespace
  • Recorded as "Unknown" (case-insensitive)

This category includes both cases where no attempt was made to capture the ID and cases where capture was attempted but the value was not available or deliberately withheld. The NMC system does not distinguish between these scenarios, so all are grouped as “missing”.

Key Findings & Interpretation

Bottom Line: Is Linkage Feasible?

ImportantLinkage Readiness Assessment

Overall SA ID completeness: 51.6% of notifications have an SA ID recorded.

Of those with SA IDs: 86.4% pass Luhn validation (valid format for linkage).

Net linkage-ready records: 44.6% of all notifications have a valid, linkable SA ID.

Assessment: 🔴 Not currently feasible for robust linkage. Fewer than half of notifications can be reliably linked. Priority: improve ID capture at point of notification.

Conditions ready for linkage (≥50% ID completeness AND ≥80% Luhn validity):

  • Tuberculosis:pulmonary: 50,783 linkable IDs (61.1% of notifications)
  • Tuberculosis:extra-pulmonary: 9,384 linkable IDs (52.8% of notifications)
  • Tuberculosis: multidrug- resistant (MDR -TB): 1,383 linkable IDs (66.1% of notifications)
  • Pertussis: 629 linkable IDs (54.5% of notifications)
  • Covid-19: 584 linkable IDs (81.9% of notifications)
  • Hepatitis C: 138 linkable IDs (70.1% of notifications)
  • Legionellosis: 105 linkable IDs (80.2% of notifications)
  • Tuberculosis: extensively drug -resistant (XDR -TB): 75 linkable IDs (69.4% of notifications)

Linkage Candidate Score

This plot ranks conditions by a composite linkage score that balances three key factors:

  1. ID Completeness (40%) — What proportion of cases have an ID recorded? Higher = more representative linked dataset.
  2. ID Validity (30%) — Of recorded IDs, what proportion pass Luhn? Higher = fewer linkage failures.
  3. Volume (30%) — How many linkable cases are available? Higher = greater statistical power and public health impact.

Note

Observation: Most conditions with low ID completeness are predominantly laboratory-notified. This is expected — laboratory notifications often lack patient identifiers when specimens are submitted without full demographic data. Interventions should focus on improving specimen submission forms and laboratory-clinician communication.

TipImproving Linkage Quality
  • SA ID not captured: Priority issue — work with facility IPC practitioners to ensure ID capture at time of notification.
  • Wrong-length IDs: Likely transcription errors; consider validation at point of entry in the NMC system.
  • Luhn failures on 13-digit IDs: Transposition errors (e.g. digit swap) — Luhn validation at point of entry would catch these immediately.
  • Folder number missing: Critical for linking back to facility clinical records; should be mandatory for hospitalised cases.
